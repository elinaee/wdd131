Portugal: A Tourist's Guide

Did you know ....

The oldest bookstore in the world: Portugal is home to the oldest bookstore in the world, Livraria Bertrand, which has been in operation since 1732.
The country of explorers: Portugal has a rich history of exploration, with famous explorers like Vasco da Gama, Ferdinand Magellan, and Bartolomeu Dias discovering new lands and sea routes.
The birthplace of the Portuguese language: The Portuguese language was born in the 12th century in the region of Galicia, which is now part of Spain. However, it was in Portugal that the language developed and evolved into the distinct dialect we know today.
The country of Fado music: Fado, a traditional Portuguese music style, is a symbol of Portuguese culture and is recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.
The land of cork: Portugal is the world's largest producer of cork, with the majority of the world's cork oak forests found in the Alentejo region.
The oldest European country: Portugal is one of the oldest countries in Europe, with its borders remaining largely unchanged since the 13th century.
The land of tiles: Portugal is famous for its beautiful azulejo tiles, which adorn the walls of buildings, churches, and palaces throughout the country.
The country of surf: Portugal has a long coastline with consistent waves, making it a popular destination for surfers from around the world.
The country of festivals: Portugal has a rich cultural calendar, with numerous festivals and celebrations throughout the year, such as the Festa de Santo António in Lisbon and the Festa do Avante! in Setúbal.
The country of gastronomy: Portuguese cuisine is a fusion of Mediterranean and Atlantic flavors, with popular dishes like bacalhau à brás, frango grelhado, and pastéis de nata.
The country of beaches: Portugal has a stunning coastline with over 1,000 km of beaches, from the golden sands of the Algarve to the rugged cliffs of the Costa Vicentina.
